# Approved Presentation Standard

This is the benchmark for reusable K-12 Science decks after the Grade 8 digestive-system revision.

## Required Flow

Each session presentation must stand alone and follow a classroom-ready arc:

1. Short student-facing target or inquiry question.
2. Main activity instructions with materials, roles, and checking points when needed.
3. Expected output or success criteria before students work independently.
4. Evidence-based discussion aligned to the lesson-plan statements.
5. Student debrief questions after major activities.
6. Independent check or exit output tied to the session objective.

## Slide Content

- Keep official objective wording in speaker notes.
- Use short learner-facing targets on slides.
- Avoid section-title slides like "Explore" or "Analyze" unless they are part of the real lesson language.
- Prefer questions, evidence checks, output criteria, and misconception repair over teacher-facing summaries.

## Visual Standard

- Use HD, accurate, necessary visuals only.
- Each discussion image must match the exact statement or claim on that slide.
- Do not repeat one generic background image across multiple discussion slides.
- For Science/STEM, prefer realistic classroom evidence visuals, accurate lab/setup images, anatomical/scientific references, or precise raster diagrams.
- Avoid cheap SVG/vector/cartoon/flat-icon visuals when they can confuse learners.
- Images should show the proof object: setup, pathway, comparison, process, expected output, misconception contrast, or model limitation.

## Student-Centered Questions

Use questions like:

- What evidence changed your first answer?
- Which part of your model is strongest?
- Which organ/process/setup is easiest to confuse?
- What does the model show, and what does it fail to show?
- Which misconception did your work correct?

## Acceptance Bar

A deck is approved only when a teacher can use it directly in class without explaining missing activity steps, replacing inaccurate images, or rewriting overloaded student slides.
